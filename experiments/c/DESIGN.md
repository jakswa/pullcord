# Experiment C — Warm & Playful

## Direction
Every transit app looks the same — dark, utilitarian, serious. This experiment asks: what if checking the bus felt *nice*?

## Key Moves

### Color
- **Brand coral** (#E85D3A) replaces cold blue — warm, energetic, visible
- **Cream canvas** (#FDF8F2) instead of white or dark — easier on eyes, feels inviting
- **Teal accents** (#0D9488) for live status — warm complement to coral
- **Warm grays** (#3B2820 family) throughout text — no cold blue-grays

### Shape & Feel
- Generous border-radius (1rem on cards) — pillowy, approachable
- Softer shadows with warm tint (rgba(61,44,32,...))
- 3px border on bus markers (slightly larger than default)
- Rounded everything — buttons, cards, inputs, badges

### Personality
- Empty state: "No buses right now. Time for a walk?" (not just "No data")
- Loading: "Checking arrivals..." (conversational, not "Loading...")
- Clock icon in empty state adds visual warmth
- The brand itself is warm — coral + cream reads as friendly, not corporate

### Map
- Light Positron tiles (matches warm light theme)
- Slightly larger bus markers (34px) for friendliness
- Warm stop pulse animation
- Softer route line treatment

### What Didn't Change
- All functionality intact — polling, tiers, staleness, click-to-focus
- Information hierarchy same (ETAs above map)
- Typography still Inter (just warmer color application)
- SVG icons throughout (no emoji)

## Philosophy
Trust comes from reliability, not from looking serious. Duolingo proved an app can be fun AND dependable. The warm palette and friendly copy don't undermine utility — they make it memorable.
